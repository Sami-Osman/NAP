Discussion Name: Was Maximilien Robespierre ultimately good for France?

1. Maximilien Robespierre was ultimately good for France.
1.1. Con: The republic he contributed to establishing isn't a good system for France.
1.1.1. Pro: The republic morally destroyed France; the Catholic values which were the cement of France were abandoned for the Republican ones.
1.1.1.1. Pro: Robespierre tried to replace Catholicism with the [Cult of the Supreme Being](https://alphahistory.com/frenchrevolution/cult-of-the-supreme-being/) - a religion based on republican values with himself at the helm.
1.1.1.2. Pro: During the revolutionary period, the Republic sought to destroy the power that Catholicism had in France through a program of [dechristianisation](https://www.iwp.edu/articles/2018/01/12/the-dechristianization-of-france-during-the-french-revolution/).
1.1.1.2.1. Pro: The Republic [supported](https://www.iwp.edu/articles/2018/01/12/the-dechristianization-of-france-during-the-french-revolution/) the destruction of Catholic crosses, statues, and plaques as well as any iconography from places of worship.
1.1.1.2.2. Pro: In 1793, the Christian calendar was [replaced](https://www.iwp.edu/articles/2018/01/12/the-dechristianization-of-france-during-the-french-revolution/) with a Republican calendar that started from the date of the Revolution.
1.1.1.2.2.1. Pro: Sundays and Christian holidays were [replaced](https://www.iwp.edu/articles/2018/01/12/the-dechristianization-of-france-during-the-french-revolution/) with festivals of Liberty, Reasons, and the Supreme Being.
1.1.2. Pro: Economically, the republic has enslaved France. The ancient system was respectful towards the laborers and protected them. The Republic instated capitalism, which results in the exact opposite.
1.1.3. Pro: Even though Napoleon heard of him during his conquests, the results of those is that France is smaller, Napoleon I lost the Flandres and Louisiane \(sold for financing losing wars\), and Napoleon III lost Alsace-Lorraine.
1.1.4. Pro: During WW1, the Republic won, but at the price of one million deaths. During WW2, France was beaten in a few weeks. Those two wars were also marked with imposed general mobilization.
1.2. Pro: He created one of the first democracies in Europe.
1.2.1. Con: The republic only lasted for 11 years.
1.2.1.1. Con: It opened ways for new democracies.
1.2.2. Con: Very few people were actually afforded the right to vote and participate in this democracy.
1.2.2.1. Pro: Despite claims of equality in this new democracy, women were [not eligible](https://www.historywiz.com/frenchrevwomen.html) to vote during the Revolutionary period.
1.2.2.1.1. Pro: The constitution of 1791 [reduced women](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Active_and_passive_citizens#) to 'passive citizens' - citizens that were entitled to protection by law but had no say in the making of these government bodies.
1.2.2.2. Pro: Strict laws on who classified as an ['active citizen'](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Active_and_passive_citizens#) in this new democracy meant that only 50,000 of the 25 million men in France were eligible to vote.
1.2.2.3. Pro: Despite the Declaration of the Rights of Free Men advocating for equality for all men, free black men were not afforded the [right to vote](https://www.historywiz.com/slavery-frenchrev.htm) until 1792.
1.3. Con: The [Reign of Terror](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reign_of_Terror) killed thousands of people.
1.3.1. Pro: More that 16,000 people were sentenced to death during the [Reign of Terror](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reign_of_Terror).
1.3.2. Pro: The [Vendean Genocide](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guerre_de_Vend%C3%A9e#%C2%AB_G%C3%A9nocide_%C2%BB_:_un_d%C3%A9bat_historique_et_juridique_r%C3%A9cent) killed 200,000 to 600,000 civilians. \(1/4 to 1/2 of the population\)
1.3.3. Con: It was neccesary to ensure the continuation of the republic.
1.3.3.1. Con: -> See 1.1.
1.3.4. Pro: Robespierre was directly responsible for the murder of thousands of people. As head of the Committee of Public Safety, he oversaw the execution of roughly [2,000 people](http://blogs.bu.edu/guidedhistory/moderneurope/robespierres-transformation-and-the-french-revolution/).
1.4. Pro: His ideas lasted for hundreds of years and were important in France and other countries in the world.
1.4.1. Pro: Without Robespierre's radical policies, the revolution may never have succeeded, and Europe might not have entered the modern era of democratic reforms.
1.4.2. Con: His ideas led to fall of the first French republic.
1.4.2.1. Con: The fall of the first French republic is due to the [French Directory](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Directory-French-history), which was not an idea of Robespierre.
1.5. Con: He caused the fall of the first French republic.
1.5.1. Con: Robespierre died in 1794, but the Republic didn't end until Napoleon's rise, nearly ten years later.
