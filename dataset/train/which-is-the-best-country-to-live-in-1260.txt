Discussion Name: Which is the best country to live in?

1. Which is the best country to live in?
1.1. Germany.
1.1.1. Con: The infrastructure, particularly the subway system or the Bundesbahn, is very backward.
1.1.1.1. Pro: The Deutsche Bahn has very high ticket prices.
1.1.1.2. Con: The subway and train system is actually much better in Germany than in a lot of other Western countries.
1.1.1.2.1. Pro: Munich has a good public transport system.
1.1.1.2.2. Pro: Berlin has a good public transport system.
1.1.2. Con: Germany suffers from [demographic change](http://www.ft.com/cms/s/0/b30c8de4-4754-11e5-af2f-4d6e0e5eda22.html) and is on the path to become a gerontocracy.
1.1.3. Con: The xenophobic AfD party is [on the rise](https://www.theguardian.com/world/video/2016/mar/14/german-anti-refugee-party-afd-celebrates-success-in-regional-elections-video) in Germany.
1.1.4. Con: [Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taxation_in_Germany#Income_tax_for_residents) has a complicated system of high taxation and dues.
1.1.5. Con: The German language is difficult to learn.
1.1.5.1. Pro: German is very complex and has lots of grammatical exceptions.
1.1.6. Pro: Germany is the [seventh most visited country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism_in_Germany) in the world and rated as one of the safest worldwide.
1.1.6.1. Pro: According to Monocle's 2018 Quality of Life Survey [Munich is the most livable city in the world](https://www.forbes.com/sites/bishopjordan/2018/06/25/monocle-most-livable-city-quality-life-survey-2018-munich/#205aac556153).
1.1.6.1.1. Pro: 4 out of the top 25 cities are in Germany.
1.1.6.2. Pro: Germany has a low rate of [intentional homicide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_intentional_homicide_rate).
1.1.7. Pro: Like other European countries Germany, has GDPR - data privacy laws to protect citizens from data theft by multinational IT companies.
1.1.7.1. Con: Germany is lagging behind in [internet](http://www.dw.com/en/germanys-high-speed-internet-is-lagging-behind/a-18313138) access.
1.1.8. Pro: Germany has a high life expectancy at [80.44 years](http://www.indexmundi.com/germany/life_expectancy_at_birth.html).
1.1.8.1. Con: Cigarette smoking is fairly common and allowed in most places.
1.1.8.1.1. Con: That is a sign of self-determination and freedom, desirable qualities in a country.
1.1.8.1.2. Con: Cigarette smoking is forbidden in train stations, public buildings, restaurants and bars in Bavaria.
1.1.8.2. Con: Canada has a high life expectancy at [81.67 years](http://www.indexmundi.com/canada/life_expectancy_at_birth.html).
1.1.8.3. Con: That is lower than in Japan \([84.46 years](http://www.indexmundi.com/japan/life_expectancy_at_birth.html)\).
1.1.9. Pro: Germany has a highly developed [social security](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_security_in_Germany) net.
1.1.9.1. Pro: Education is for free and unburdens parents financially.
1.1.9.1.1. Pro: Primary and secondary education is free of charge.
1.1.9.1.2. Pro: Most German universities are public institutions, funded by the Länder governments, where students can study with no cost.
1.1.10. Pro: Germany is considered the economic leader of the EU.
1.1.10.1. Con: [House price-to-Income Ratio](http://www.imf.org/external/research/housing/) is high in Germany compared to other Western European countries.
1.1.11. Pro: Germans have a good work-life balance.
1.1.11.1. Pro: Many companies give up to 6 weeks of vacation time.
1.1.11.2. Pro: Germany has a unique system of paid parental leave, financially supported by the state.
1.1.11.3. Pro: Germany has [4 weeks of federally mandated vacation time](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bundesurlaubsgesetz#Gesetzlicher_Mindesturlaub).
1.1.11.4. Pro: On average Germans work [35.6 hours per week](http://www.theguardian.com/news/datablog/2011/dec/08/europe-working-hours).
1.2. United States.
1.2.1. Pro: The average [size of houses](http://www.forbes.com/sites/bruceupbin/2011/07/26/apartment-size-around-the-world-infographic/#43902007c79d) in the US is the biggest in the world. Thus, living in the US means you have a lot of living space.
1.2.1.1. Con: However, estimates say that there are [1.6 million](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homelessness_in_the_United_States#Annual_Homeless_Assessment_Report_to_Congress) homeless people in the US.
1.2.1.2. Con: Having lots of living space is not necessary as long as it is well connected to all necessary amenities nearby.
1.2.2. Pro: The US is ethnically and culturally diverse.
1.2.2.1. Pro: The U.S. is physically large and has a diversity of cities \(each with their own characteristics\) to choose from.
1.2.2.2. Pro: The US is one of the [most](http://www.livescience.com/28945-american-culture.html) culturally diverse countries in the world.
1.2.2.2.1. Pro: Nearly every region of the world has influenced American culture, as it is a country of immigrants.
1.2.2.2.2. Pro: US culture has been shaped by the cultures of Native Americans, Latin Americans, Africans and Asians.
1.2.2.3. Pro: The [US Census Bureau](http://www.census.gov/prod/2013pubs/acs-22.pdf) estimates that more than 300 languages are spoken in the United States.
1.2.2.4. Pro: Nearly every known religion is practiced in the US, which was founded on the basis of religious freedom.
1.2.2.4.1. Con: However, the absolute majority, 77% of Americans, identify themselves as [Christians](http://www.gallup.com/poll/159548/identify-christian.aspx).
1.2.2.5. Pro: American cuisine has been influenced by Europeans and Native Americans in its early history.
1.2.2.6. Pro: The US is widely known around the world as a leader in mass media production, including television and movies.
1.2.2.6.1. Pro: According to the U.S. Department of Commerce, the US comprises [one-third](https://www.selectusa.gov/media-entertainment-industry-united-states) of the worldwide media and entertainment industry.
1.2.2.6.2. Pro: The US has a vibrant movie industry, centered in Hollywood, California, and American movies are popular worldwide.
1.2.3. Con: Xenophobic right-wing extremism is [on the rise](https://www.propublica.org/article/global-right-wing-extremism-networks-are-growing-the-u-s-is-just-now-catching-up) in the US.
1.2.4. Con: Employees in the US have less days of paid vacation than most other countries.
1.2.4.1. Pro: There is [no statutory](https://www.dol.gov/general/topic/workhours/vacation_leave) minimum paid vacation or paid public holidays. It is left to the employers to offer paid vacation.
1.2.4.1.1. Pro: Some employers offer [no vacation at all](https://shrm.org/Research/SurveyFindings/Documents/14-0301%20Benefits_Report_TEXT_FNL.pdf).
1.2.5. Con: A healthcare system based on a for profit platform is morally wrong
1.2.5.1. Con: -> See discussion #16319: Health care should be voluntary and market provided, not mandatory, nor provided or regulated by government.
1.2.5.2. Con: Claiming anything is "morally wrong" is too broad a statement. People can have different morals, and many people feel a capitalist healthcare system is the right path.
1.2.6. Pro: The US grants a wide amount of constitutional rights for the individual.
1.2.6.1. Con: The US allows federal institutions to arbitrarily deny those constitutional rights in the name of security and the "war against terrorism".
1.2.6.2. Pro: The US constitutionally guarantees the right to bear arms.
1.2.6.2.1. Con: Only if you are a [well regulated militia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution#Text).
1.2.6.2.2. Con: Not everyone agrees with [America's gun culture](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-41488081), therefore a constitutional right to bear arms could be also seen as a disadvantage.
1.2.6.3. Pro: The US constitutionally guarantees the right to the almost completely unrestricted freedom of speech.
1.2.7. Pro: Living in the US comes with an array of economic benefits.
1.2.7.1. Pro: The US has one of the world's lowest marginal income tax rates, at [27%](http://www.investopedia.com/financial-edge/1011/countries-with-the-highest-and-lowest-taxes.aspx).
1.2.7.2. Pro: In the US there are more possibilities for entrepreneurs to open up businesses.
1.2.7.2.1. Pro: The [American Dream](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Dream#Public_opinion) is still quite alive.
1.2.7.2.1.1. Pro: The US economy offers a lot of possibilities to realise business opportunities that would not get a chance anywhere else.
1.2.7.2.1.2. Con: US social mobility fell by more than [70%](https://medium.com/swlh/the-american-dream-is-dead-fe9290dd0516) in the past half-century.
1.2.7.2.1.2.1. Pro: In 2016, about half of 30-year-olds earned less than their parents at the same age.
1.2.7.2.2. Pro: -> See 1.2.4.1.
1.2.7.3. Pro: The average income in the United States is one of the highest in the world.
1.2.7.3.1. Con: Despite this the [distribution of wealth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wealth_inequality_in_the_United_States#/media/File:Wealth_distribution_by_percentile_in_the_United_States.png) in the US is highly skewed towards a very small minority which implies that the high average income is not sufficient to grow wealth for the average citizen.
1.2.8. Con: The US is an unsafe and unhealthy country to live in.
1.2.8.1. Pro: The U.S. has high obesity rates.
1.2.8.1.1. Pro: More than one-third \([35.7%](http://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/health-statistics/Pages/overweight-obesity-statistics.aspx)\) of adults in the US are considered to be obese.
1.2.8.1.1.1. Pro: Almost 3 in 4 men \(74%\) are considered to be overweight or obese.
1.2.8.1.1.2. Pro: About 8% of women are considered to have extreme obesity.
1.2.8.1.2. Pro: Being overweight and obese are [risk factors](http://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/health-statistics/Pages/overweight-obesity-statistics.aspx) for type 2 diabetes, heart disease, high blood pressure and other health problems.
1.2.8.2. Pro: The U.S. has a [high rate of gun deaths](http://www.npr.org/sections/goatsandsoda/2015/12/07/458815891/the-u-s-is-a-world-leader-in-gun-deaths).
1.2.8.3. Pro: The U.S. is frequently involved in wars.
1.2.8.3.1. Pro: The US started the [Iraq War](http://www.britannica.com/event/Iraq-War) in 2003, also known as the Second Persian Gulf War, which lasted until 2011.
1.2.8.3.2. Pro: From 2001 - 2014 the US was involved in the [Afghanistan War](http://www.britannica.com/event/Afghanistan-War), that was triggered by the 9/11 attacks.
1.2.8.3.3. Pro: Being considered a citizen of a warmongering country feels bad.
1.2.8.3.4. Con: Most Americans are [indifferent](https://www.stratfor.com/weekly/american-publics-indifference-foreign-affairs) about foreign policy. They most likely do not feel any effects of the US' involvement in wars.
1.2.8.3.4.1. Con: In a CDC [survey](http://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/mm5135a2.htm) on the psychological effects of 9/11, three fourths \(75%\) of respondents reported having problems attributed to the attacks. Nearly half \(48%\) of respondents reported that they experienced anger after the attacks.
1.2.9. Con: The U.S. is falling behind other countries in education.
1.2.9.1. Pro: As of 2014 a [Gallup poll](http://www.gallup.com/poll/170822/believe-creationist-view-human-origins.aspx) found that 42% of US citizens believe in Creationist views of human origins.
1.2.9.2. Pro: According to the OECD's global education ranking, American students of 2011-2012 ranked only [28th](http://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/singapore/singapore-tops-oecd-s/1843546.html) in math and science  compared with students in 76 other countries. Singapore, Japan, Switzerland, Netherlands and Canada made it into the top 10.
1.2.10. Con: The US has a very individualistic culture that can have a lot of disadvantages.
1.2.10.1. Pro: Individualistic cultures may be worse at responding to pandemic.
1.2.10.2. Pro: This culture is self-oriented and not responsible for others needs what makes it difficult to live in such an environment.
1.2.11. Con: They stopped protecting some basic rights.
1.2.11.1. Pro: The right to abortion is not [protected anymore](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-54513499).
1.3. Uruguay.
1.3.1. Pro: The [climate](http://hubpages.com/travel/expatconfessions) is Southern sub-tropical, with the coastal areas moderated by Rio de la Plata and the Atlantic ocean. This means summer is comfortably warm and winter is mild and humid.
1.3.2. Pro: Uruguay is very environment friendly and at the same time electricity costs are low as well.
1.3.2.1. Pro: Nearly [95%](http://www.greentechmedia.com/articles/read/uruguay-now-gets-95-of-its-electricity-from-renewables) of Uruguay's electricity comes from renewable energy. The [dramatic shift](http://www.greentechmedia.com/articles/read/uruguay-now-gets-95-of-its-electricity-from-renewables), taking less than ten years and without government funding, lowered electricity costs and slashed the country's carbon footprint.
1.3.3. Pro: Uruguay no longer imports electricity, so it is [energy-independent](http://www.sciencealert.com/uruguay-has-shifted-to-getting-95-of-its-electricity-from-clean-energy-sources-in-less-than-10-years).
1.3.4. Pro: A 2010 [Latinobarómetro poll](http://www.economist.com/node/17627929) found that, within Latin America, Uruguayans are among the most supportive of democracy and by far the most satisfied with the way democracy works in their country.
1.3.5. Pro: [Education](http://idbdocs.iadb.org/wsdocs/getdocument.aspx?docnum=35521105) in Uruguay is secular, free and compulsory for 14 years, starting at the age of 4.
1.3.6. Con: Uruguay has a [negative S&P credit rating](http://www.focus-economics.com/countries/uruguay).
1.3.7. Con: Uruguay has a [negative GDP](https://www.economy.com/dismal/countries/IURY) \(-0.5% as of 2016Q1\).
1.3.8. Con: Uruguay has a [high crime rate](https://www.osac.gov/pages/ContentReportDetails.aspx?cid=17245).

1.4.2.1. Pro: Israeli cuisine is great for [vegetarians](http://www.ynetnews.com/articles/0,7340,L-3850942,00.html).
1.4.3. Con: With 17% on all the products and services the value-added tax \([VAT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_tax_rates)\) rate is very high.

1.5. South Africa.
