Discussion Name: Should taxes pay for professional sports stadiums?

1. Taxes should pay for professional sports stadiums.
1.1. Pro: Sport is considered [a human right](http://www.bbc.co.uk/ethics/sport/fair/rights.shtml).
1.1.1. Con: Even without government funding, your right to play or watch sports is not taken away
1.1.2. Con: Professional sports stadiums are not a human right
1.2. Con: Stadiums are not a [common good](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_good) because they do not benefit the large majority of taxpayers.
1.3. Pro: Sport stadiums lead to increased traffic in the area.
1.3.1. Con: Increased traffic is not a good idea, namely due to noxious fumes being added to the air within the area.
1.4. Con: Private investors are better at building high-quality stadiums as they're often willing to spend more money on it.
1.5. Con: It diverts funds away from essential government services such as infrastructure, education, health care and etc.
1.6. Pro: Sport stadiums work as advertisement for the city.
1.6.1. Pro: The fame of major soccer teams, such as FC Barcelona or Bayern Munich carries the names of the city.
1.6.2. Con: If the team is not good, it might negatively impact the reputation of the city
1.7. Con: Sport stadiums erected in the context of mega events are often to the detriment of the cities.
1.7.1. Pro: An estimated [1.5 million people](http://www.ruig-gian.org/ressources/Report%20Fair%20Play%20FINAL%20FINAL%20070531.pdf) were kicked out of their homes to make room for Olympic construction in Beijing.
1.7.2. Con: In Munich, the Olympic park is still in [heavy use](https://www.olympiapark.de/en/olympiapark-munich/).
1.7.3. Con: The British Olympic Stadium statue was [repurposed for an entertaining ride](https://www.dailymail.co.uk/travel/travel_news/article-3656378/London-s-Olympic-statue-turned-world-s-biggest-tunnel-slide-MailOnline-dares-dizzying-drops-40-second-descent.html).
1.7.3.1. Con: The ArcelorMittal Orbit in London's Olympic Park is [one of London's ugliest buildings](https://www.telegraph.co.uk/travel/destinations/europe/united-kingdom/england/london/galleries/london-ugliest-buildings/arcelormittal-orbit/).
1.7.3.2. Con: The orbit has been recording a loss due to [lack of visitors](https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2015/oct/20/olympic-parks-orbit-tower-costing-taxpayer-10000-a-week).
1.7.3.2.1. Pro: The sculpture-turned-ride is in [Â£13 million debt](https://www.art-critique.com/en/2019/09/arcelormittal-orbit-slides-into-debt/) as its popularity has slid in the years following London Olympics.
1.7.3.3. Pro: ArcelorMittal Orbit was voted [one of 2019's coolest visits](https://time.com/collection/worlds-coolest-places-2019/5736001/arcelormittal-orbit-london-united-kingdom/) for children.
1.8. Con: Revenue from ticket sales or other profits from the team and stadium is not shared with the city.
1.8.1. Con: This is because the stadiums are not public yet. Once that taxes will be used to build them, there will be agreements with the teams to gain a revenue on the tickets.
1.9. Con: Businesses have been hurt by the addition of stadiums to the area.
1.10. Con: New stadiums are demanded at regular intervals and teams will threaten/leave if not fulfilled.
1.11. Pro: Sport stadiums allow for community activities, like concerts or rodeo events.
1.11.1. Con: Not all taxpayers enjoy visiting sport stadiums.
1.12. Con: Sport is a distraction to the public.
1.12.1. Con: It is beneficial for the public to occasionally distract themselves with leisure activities like watching sports.
