1. What are the universal amendments that should exist in any constitutional laws of all countries? Please suggest constitutional amendments!
1.1. \[Freedom of expression, arts and sciences\] Every person shall have the right freely to express and disseminate his opinions in speech, writing and pictures and to inform himself without hindrance from generally accessible sources. Freedom of the press and freedom of reporting by means of broadcasts and films shall be guaranteed. There shall be no censorship.
1.1.1. Con: Freedom of expression can be abused by hatred and lies. There should be a limit for it since it can hurt other people.
1.1.1.1. Pro: Protection against hate speech and comments should be added alongside the guaranteed freedom of expression \([p. 15](https://www.loc.gov/law/help/freedom-expression/limits-expression.pdf)\).
1.1.2. Pro: Freedom of expression is the most important human right to protect us against any totalitarian and dictatorship.
1.2. \[Human Rights\] - Human dignity shall be inviolable. To respect and protect. it shall be the duty of all state authority.
1.2.1. Con: Giving states the authority to uphold human rights can lead to drastically different interpretations by different governments.
1.2.1.1. Con: The interpretation of human rights should be clearly stated within the constitutional laws and given to the constitutional court of law in case of controversial subjects or referendum.
1.2.1.2. Con: More ammandments needed in the parent thesis to guarantee the human rights within the sociaty and the government should be the best example of preservation of those rights as am agent
1.2.2. Pro: Guaranteeing the inviolability of human dignity will benefit those most at risk of persecution.
1.2.2.1. Con: Even if the constitution of a country includes the provision for the inviolability of human dignity, it is not guaranteed that vulnerable groups would be protected.
1.2.2.1.1. Pro: The Constitution of the US [guarantees human dignity](https://scholarship.law.georgetown.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1112&context=facpub) via the [8th Amendment](https://constitution.congress.gov/constitution/amendment-8/) \(p. 17\). However, black populations within the US are still [persecuted](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2017/aug/11/chokehold-police-black-men-paul-butler-race-america).
1.3. \[Right to Property\] Every person shall have the right to acquire, hold and dispose of property in any part of the country they are a resident or citizen of.
1.4. \[Personal freedoms\] Every person shall have the right to free development of his personality insofar as he does not violate the rights of others or offend against the constitutional order or the moral law.
1.4.1. Pro: Personal Freedom is a crucial form of freedom to guaranty the free will of humans.
1.5. \[Equality before the law\] All persons shall be equal before the law. Men and women shall have equal rights. The state shall promote the actual implementation of equal rights for women and men and take steps to eliminate disadvantages that now exist.
1.5.1. Pro: Any Gender-based discrimination should be condemned and acting against it should be supported on a constitutional level.
1.5.1.1. Con: Many religious populations would not accept this amendment and are likely to protest against them.
1.5.2. Con: This law which belongs to exisiting german constitution doesn't include Non-binary aspect of gender into account.
1.5.3. Con: Many constitutions already have a provision guaranteeing equality before the law, but the application of this provision is [discriminatory](http://www.ruthlesscriticism.com/equality.htm).
1.6. \[Equality before the law\] No person shall be favored or disfavoured because of sex, parentage, appearance, language, homeland and origin, faith or religious or political opinions. No person shall be disfavoured because of disability.
1.6.1. Pro: This amendment guarantees and supports the livelihood of minorities and respects diversity.
