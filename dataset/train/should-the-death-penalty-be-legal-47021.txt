Discussion Name: Should the death penalty be legal?

1. The death penalty should be legal.
1.1. Pro: Sometimes, there is little doubt that people have committed treacherous crimes, and the death penalty is the best way to punish those crimes.
1.2. Pro: A lot of the time, the survivors and family members of people who were murdered or attacked in other violent ways want to see capital punishment done as justice to the people who attacked them or their family members.
1.2.1. Con: Justice should be based on the law, not on the desires of the victims or their families.
1.2.2. Pro: [These families](https://www.wbur.org/onpoint/2019/03/20/death-penalty-california-murder-victims-families) of the murder victims say the death penalty would aid in the closure.
1.3. Con: The death penalty has no deterrent effect. It is an unusual punishment. E.g.- if somebody commits assault, their punishment is not assault on them.
1.3.1. Con: The results of [several \(economic\) papers](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/237588087_Statistical_Evidence_on_Capital_Punishment_and_the_Deterrence_of_Homicide) suggest that the death penalty deters murder.
1.3.2. Pro: Top experts doubt the [deterrent effect](https://deathpenaltyinfo.org/policy-issues/deterrence) of the death penalty.
1.3.3. Pro: Top experts have said that the death penalty is not proven to deter murder. [DPIC](https://deathpenaltyinfo.org/policy-issues/deterrence)
1.4. Con: The death penalty is not efficient in rising overall happiness.
1.4.1. Pro: The [Utilitarian theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utilitarianism) supports this belief.
1.5. Con: The National Academy of Sciences performed a study that concluded 4% of death row inmates are innocent. The death penalty is extremely flawed in that it allows innocent people to be killed.
1.6. Pro: The death penalty is the implementation of justice.
1.6.1. Con: In the case of wrongful conviction, the execution of the death penalty cannot be reversed.
1.6.2. Con: The death penalty categorically rejects the possibility of rehabilitation.
1.7. Con: The death penalty is immoral because it involves taking a life when there are other options.
1.8. Con: The death penalty in the United States discriminates against the mentally ill, racial minorities, and people who cannot afford expensive legal representation.
1.8.1. Pro: One study [found](http://www.worldcoalition.org/media/resourcecenter/EN_WD2017_FactSheet)(In the United States, in 2007, according to the Equal Justice Initiative, 95% of people on death row have disadvantaged economic backgrounds.) that 95% of people on death row in the United States were from disadvantaged economic backgrounds.
1.8.2. Con: This discrimination is primarily an argument against the US judicial system, not the death penalty.
