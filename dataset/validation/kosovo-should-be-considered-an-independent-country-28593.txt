Discussion Name: Kosovo should be considered an independent country.

1. Kosovo should be considered an independent country.
1.1. Con: Recognition of Kosovo as independent country might lead other regions to seek independence in other countries.
1.1.1. Con: All people have a [right to self-determination.](https://unpo.org/article/4957)
1.1.1.1. Pro: More decentralism will make the world a better place, because the rulers of large, powerful countries are more likely to be out of touch with the people under their control and to threaten their neighbors, while more smaller countries would mean more local control and more accountability of governments to the people.
1.1.1.1.1. Pro: If Kosovars had felt that their views were being represented in the Serbian government, they wouldn't have declared independence.
1.1.2. Con: Independence movements aren't inherently bad.
1.1.2.1. Pro: The American Revolution is generally considered to have been a good idea.
1.1.2.2. Con: Some revolutions and independence movements were extraordinarily brutal, bloody and horrific and could be considered the worst points for some countries.
1.1.2.2.1. Pro: The French revolution was extraordinarily brutal, not only was the guillotine a horrible way of killing people, and it killed a lot of people, and the next period of french history wasn't that good either. Many [brutal](https://listverse.com/2015/06/18/10-horrific-episodes-from-one-of-historys-bloodiest-revolutions/) and [disgusting things](https://www.ranker.com/list/french-revolution-bloodiest-moments/setareh-janda) happened in it even though it helped France become the diplomatic entity it is today.
1.1.3. Pro: There are [dozens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_active_separatist_movements_in_Europe) of active separatist movements in Europe. Encouraging them to secede would create political havoc on the continent.
1.1.3.1. Con: If someone or something \(such as the European Union\) claims to support democracy, they shouldn't be against democratically decided upon independence.
1.2. Pro: The [majority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_recognition_of_Kosovo) of UN, EU, and NATO countries view Kosovo as independent.
1.2.1. Pro: Kosovo's [democracy score](https://freedomhouse.org/report/nations-transit/2017/kosovo) has slowly but steadily improved over the past ten years.
1.2.2. Pro: American president Joe Biden has [called](https://www.aa.com.tr/en/americas/biden-urges-serbia-to-recognize-kosovo/2136860) on Serbia to recognize Kosovo's independence.
1.3. Con: Kosovo has not obtained its independence by its own forces, but by a third party intervening on its behalf since 1999 - the US led NATO coalition. Sovereignty is earned, not given.
1.3.1. Con: According to international law, sovereignty can be acquired in [multiple â€‹ways](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acquisition_of_sovereignty).
1.4. Con: It would be considered an act of aggression and disrespect to Serbia.
1.4.1. Pro: Serbia and Kosovo have a [long history](https://www.euronews.com/2019/05/28/a-history-of-tension-serbia-kosovo-relations-explained) of conflict.
1.4.2. Con: This independence claim isn't considered an internal issue like Catalonia's independence claim is.
1.5. Pro: Kosovo's independence is [legal](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-serbia-kosovo/kosovo-independence-declaration-deemed-legal-idUSTRE66L01720100722) under international law.
1.5.1. Pro: Kosovo is a [member](https://2009-2017.state.gov/r/pa/prs/ps/2009/06a/125489.htm) of the IMF and World Bank.
1.6. Pro: The majority of people in Kosovo want to be independent.
1.6.1. Pro: In an [independence referendum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1991_Kosovan_independence_referendum) in 1991, 99% of voters voted for independence.
1.6.1.1. Con: This vote was not flawless.The majority of the Kosovo Albanian's may have wanted independence or further autonomy, but this vote did not include all of Kosovo's populace and excluded a lot of Serbs.
1.7. Con: It could be considered disrespect towards Yugoslavia's history, break up and subsequent wars.
1.7.1. Pro: History often plays a role in determining sovereignty.
1.7.1.1. Pro: Taiwan's [political status](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_status_of_Taiwan) remains undecided, despite being a de facto independent nation for 70 years, due to its history with China.
1.7.2. Con: Other countries have been broken up and new countries declared independent without regard for disrespecting history.
1.7.2.1. Pro: India was [partitioned](https://theconversation.com/how-the-partition-of-india-happened-and-why-its-effects-are-still-felt-today-81766) into India and Pakistan, despite the numerous problems it has caused.
1.7.3. Pro: If Kosovo had wanted independence, surely they would have fought a war, or negotiated, to get independence during the split of Yugoslavia
1.7.3.1. Con: The wish for independence can manifest at any time, not just in times of war.
1.8. Pro: It was [autonomously governed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialist_Autonomous_Province_of_Kosovo) during the soviet Yugoslavia period of history.
1.9. Pro: People in Kosovo were [murdered by Serbian state apparatus](https://1997-2001.state.gov/global/human_rights/kosovoii/homepage.html) just because they were of a different ethnicity; that is why it was necessary for NATO to intervene and help it to be a independent country.
