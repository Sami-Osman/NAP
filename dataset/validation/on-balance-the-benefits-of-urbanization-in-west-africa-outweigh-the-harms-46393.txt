Discussion Name: On balance, the benefits of urbanization in West Africa outweigh the harms

1. On balance, the benefits of West African urbanization outweigh the harms.
1.1. Con: Urbanization increases urban-rural opportunity inequalities.
1.2. Pro: Urbanization leads to a better distribution of goods.
1.2.1. Pro: Urbanization can contribute to lower distribution costs, allowing for an increase in the distribution of goods [\(pg. 3](http://file:///C:/Users/miada/AppData/Local/Temp/PGDA_WP_119.pdf)\).
1.3. Con: Urbanization increases poverty.
1.3.1. Con: Several studies have shown that urbanized areas experience [lower levels](https://www.brookings.edu/blog/africa-in-focus/2017/09/01/figure-of-the-week-urbanization-poverty-and-social-protection-in-east-africa/)(Several studies cited in the report highlight that in sub-Saharan Africa rural areas tend to have higher poverty rates in urban areas\nand that higher levels of urbanization are linked to lower poverty and higher GDP rates globally.) of poverty than rural areas.
1.3.2. Con: Urbanization adds more [job opportunities](https://richmondvale.org/en/blog/effects-of-urbanization-on-poverty) for rural workers, thus decreasing poverty.
1.3.2.1. Con: In Africa, urbanization isn't taking place because of increased job prospects, [it's taking place despite a lack of job opportunities](https://www.worldbank.org/en/news/feature/2016/06/17/looking-at-urbanization-through-a-jobs-lens).
1.4. Pro: Urbanized West African cities provide better education than non-urbanized areas.
1.4.1. Pro: Urbanization will increase access to better educational opportunities, which will improve quality of life in the long term \([p. 48](https://www.afdb.org/en/documents/west-africa-economic-outlook-2020-coping-covid-19-pandemic)\).
1.5. Pro: Urbanization results in better social outcomes.
1.5.1. Pro: There is [increasing support for gender equality](https://oxfamblogs.org/fp2p/support-for-gender-equality-is-growing-but-why-is-this-mostly-in-urban-areas/) in urban areas.
1.6. Con: Increased urbanization in West Africa harms the environment.
1.6.1. Pro: Urbanization leads to increased [deforestation](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1748-9326/aa94fe).
1.6.1.1. Pro: Because of urbanization, there is a higher demand for food in cities. In order to meet this demand, [farmers have converted forests into agricultural fields](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1748-9326/aa94fe).
1.6.1.2. Pro: Diseases Spread Fast
1.6.1.2.1. Pro: Urbanization leads to a faster [spread of infectious diseases](http://theconversation.com/outbreaks-like-coronavirus-start-in-and-spread-from-the-edges-of-cities-130666#:~:text=Rapid%20urbanization%20enables%20the%20spread,-to-human%20species%20boundary.), especially in peripheral areas.
1.6.2. Con: Since African cities are expanding, they can be set on "a greener development path". \([p. 37](https://africancityenergy.org/uploads/resource_143.pdf)\)
