Discussion Name: Shamima Begum case: Should she retain her British Citizenship?

1. Shamima Begum case: She should retain her British Citizenship.
1.1. Pro: She is a British citizen and is the responsibility of the UK.
1.1.1. Con: As she has broken the laws of another country she should face trial there otherwise we are undermining the justice system of another nation.
1.1.2. Pro: The UK government failed to prevent the [radicalization](https://www.oxfordstudent.com/2019/03/03/shamima-begum-is-britains-responsibility/) of a vulnerable child.
1.1.2.1. Pro: By allowing her to be a British citizen and letting her return to the UK, she will have a chance at being [de-radicalized.](https://www.oxfordstudent.com/2019/03/03/shamima-begum-is-britains-responsibility/)
1.2. Con: She is a potential threat to the security of the UK.
1.2.1. Con: She can share valuable information about recruitment efforts among British youth.
1.2.1.1. Con: The bigger threat comes from continuing her ideology and visiting that on her children.
1.3. Pro: She was a child of age fifteen when she made her decision to join ISIS.
1.3.1. Pro: The Metropolitan Police has taken [responsibility and has subsequently apologized](https://ukhumanrightsblog.com/2019/03/01/shamina-begum-is-stripping-her-of-her-citizenship-the-right-response/) to the families for failing to warn them that the schoolgirls were at risk and suggested that they would not face criminal charges if they returned to the United Kingdom.
1.3.1.1. Con: The [Metropolitan Police](http://Syria girls: Met Police chief apologises for letter failure http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-31820783) has categorically NOT taken responsibility.
1.3.2. Con: Most girls of age fifteen are itching to leave school, earn money, buy clothes and go on holiday. They are seldom thinking marriage and babies. She is hence not a child in any normal sense.
1.3.3. Pro: She should be given another opportunity in life.
1.4. Con: She effectively renounced her citizenship when she left the UK to join ISIS.
1.4.1. Con: There is no law that automatically revokes your citizenship when you join a terror group.
1.4.2. Pro: She Joined a terrorist organization \(ISIS\) and as such committed treason, thereby forfeiting her right to be a citizen of Britain.
1.4.3. Con: It is illegal to [render an individual stateless](https://www.oxfordstudent.com/2019/03/03/shamima-begum-is-britains-responsibility/) under international law.
1.5. Con: By allowing her to return, Britain may be encouraging others to follow in her footsteps.
1.5.1. Con: Well, these jihadi brides seemingly have a right to return and not much can be done about it under international citizenship laws. Then problem is what to do with them all and to discourage their returning.
1.6. Pro: If she is allowed to return, she will have to face British justice.
1.6.1. Con: This would require the justice office to go to a war zone to actually collect any evidence of a crime. [Or rely on the local governments to provide the necessary information](https://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/middle-east/shamima-begum-isis-syria-morality-police-suicide-belts-a8869016.html).
