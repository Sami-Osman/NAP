Discussion Name: Is America really "Land of the Free"?

1. Is America truly "land of the free"?
1.1. Con: States that are making Abortions illegal are obstructing peoples personal freedom
1.1.1. Pro: Limiting abortion access further compromises the US's already precarious [healthcare system](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/crsj/publications/human_rights_magazine_home/the-state-of-healthcare-in-the-united-states/health-care-as-a-human-right/), which limits individuals freedom significantly.
1.2. Con: Paying Taxes.
1.2.1. Pro: Without paying taxes, the US government could not continue to defend individual freedoms.
1.2.1.1. Pro: Taxes support the US military, which defends the US from threats to national security, which is essential for preserving the American way of life.
1.2.2. Con: If you don't pay taxes you also can get your Individual freedoms taken away
1.3. Con: The United States is home to more incarcerated individuals than any other nation.
1.3.1. Con: Many of these people are incarcerated because they engaged in crimes that hindered the freedom of others. Thus, America is made more free by their incarceration.
1.3.2. Pro: More people [per capita](https://www.prisonpolicy.org/reports/pie2022.html) \(573 in 100,000\) are incarcerated in the US than any other country.
1.4. Con: Increased violence on a [social and institutional level](https://english.news.cn/20220228/3120e00203a94a86a85fd8ee748e1053/c.html) has led to a threat to individual safety so severe that it compromises individual freedom.
1.4.1. Pro: High levels of violence may compromise individual's freedom of movement, if they feel unsafe to travel.
1.4.2. Pro: Institutional violence can have a chilling effect on the freedom to protest or publicly challenge policies and practices.
1.5. Pro: America has some of the greatest economic freedoms in the world.
1.5.1. Con: The majority of America is enslaved in a poverty trap due to the economic wealth inequalities.
1.6. Pro: Freedom of expression is a pillar of American society.
1.6.1. Pro: Freedom of expression is guaranteed by the [first amendment](https://www.law.cornell.edu/constitution/first_amendment)(Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof; or abridging the freedom of speech, or of the press; or the right of the people peaceably to assemble, and to petition the government for a redress of grievances.) of the US constitution.
1.6.2. Con: There are still sizeable [limitations](https://www.uscourts.gov/about-federal-courts/educational-resources/about-educational-outreach/activity-resources/what-does) on freedom of expression in America.
1.7. Con: America's healthcare system is not monetarily free at the point of use.
1.8. Pro: We freely give up some of rights in exchange for protection and freedom.
1.8.1. Con: Some Americans are deprived of their rights without their consent.
1.8.1.1. Pro: Death row inmates are deprived of their right to life, usually against their will.
1.9. Pro: America is one of the most religiously free countries in the world.
1.9.1. Con: Religious freedom includes freedom from religion. Yet, [atheists](https://www.pewresearch.org/religion/2019/07/23/feelings-toward-religious-groups/) are poorly regarded in America.
1.9.2. Pro: The [US constitution](https://www.law.cornell.edu/constitution/articlevi) expressly rules out a religious test for public office.
